📘 1. Paragraph Structure: The TEEEC Model

A well-structured paragraph is the foundation of excellent writing. The TEEEC model provides a clear framework for creating coherent, developed paragraphs that fully address the topic.

📌 Understanding TEEEC

📝 TEEEC = Topic Sentence + Explanation + Example + Explanation + Concluding Sentence

T - Topic Sentence: The main idea of the paragraph (what you want to say)

E - Explanation: Explain and develop the topic sentence

E - Example: Provide a specific example to illustrate your point

E - Explanation: Explain the significance of the example

C - Concluding Sentence: Restate or summarize the main idea

📌 The Topic Sentence

The topic sentence is the most important sentence in a paragraph. It:

  • Introduces the main idea
  • Is usually the first sentence (but can be elsewhere)
  • Is general enough to cover the whole paragraph
  • Is specific enough to be meaningful
// Good vs Bad Topic Sentences
// Good Topic Sentence:
"Social media has both positive and negative effects on young people."
This is clear, introduces the main idea, and sets up the paragraph.

// Bad Topic Sentence:
"Social media is popular."
Too vague — doesn't indicate what the paragraph will discuss

// Bad Topic Sentence:
"Social media was invented in 2004."
Too narrow — doesn't allow for development and discussion

📌 Complete TEEEC Paragraph Example

// TEEEC Paragraph: Social Media and Communication
// Topic Sentence (T)
Social media has revolutionized the way people communicate with each other.

// Explanation 1 (E)
Through various platforms, individuals can now stay connected with friends and family regardless of geographical distance.

// Example (E)
For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, many families used video calling apps like Zoom and FaceTime to maintain regular contact.

// Explanation of Example (E)
This allowed grandparents to see their grandchildren and friends to celebrate birthdays together, despite being physically separated during lockdowns.

// Concluding Sentence (C)
Therefore, social media has made communication more accessible and convenient in our modern world.

📌 Another TEEEC Example

// TEEEC Paragraph: Online Education
// Topic Sentence (T)
Online education has made learning more accessible than ever before.

// Explanation 1 (E)
Students from remote areas or developing countries can now access courses from top universities without traveling.

// Example (E)
For example, platforms like Coursera and edX offer courses from Harvard, MIT, and other prestigious institutions for free or at low cost.

// Explanation of Example (E)
This means that someone in a small village in Tunisia can learn computer programming from the best instructors in the world.

// Concluding Sentence (C)
In conclusion, online education has democratized access to knowledge and created new opportunities for learners worldwide.

📌 Alternative Paragraph Models

PEE Model (Simpler)

P - Point: State your main idea

E - Evidence: Provide supporting evidence or example

E - Explanation: Explain how the evidence supports your point

TEA Model (Even Simpler)

T - Topic: Introduce the topic

E - Evidence: Provide evidence or examples

A - Analysis: Analyze what this means or why it matters

✅ Tips for Strong Paragraphs

• Each paragraph should focus on ONE main idea

• Keep paragraphs balanced in length (usually 4-8 sentences)

• Use transition sentences to connect paragraphs

• Include specific examples rather than general statements

• Avoid starting multiple paragraphs the same way

📗 2. The Writing Process: Planning and Drafting

Good writing doesn't happen in one draft. Following a systematic process will help you produce well-organized, coherent pieces of writing.

📌 The 5-Stage Writing Process

Stage 1: Analyze the Task

// Before Writing: Understand What is Asked
// Ask yourself:
• What type of writing is required? (email, essay, article?)
• Who is my audience? (friend, teacher, employer?)
• What is the purpose? (inform, persuade, entertain?)
• How long should it be? (word limit?)
• What format is expected? (formal, informal?)

Stage 2: Brainstorm and Plan

// Planning Techniques
// Mind Map:
Write the topic in the center and branch out with related ideas.

// List:
Write down all ideas, then organize them into groups.

// Outline:
Create a structured plan with main points and sub-points.

Stage 3: Draft (First Version)

// While Drafting
// DO:
• Write quickly to capture your ideas
• Focus on content, not perfection
• Use your outline as a guide
• Include examples and details

// DON'T:
• Worry about spelling or grammar yet
• Edit as you write
• Second-guess your ideas

Stage 4: Revise

// Revision Checklist
// Content:
• Have I addressed all parts of the question?
• Is my argument clear and logical?
• Do I have enough supporting examples?
• Is the structure clear?

// Organization:
• Do my ideas flow logically?
• Are transitions smooth?
• Is each paragraph focused on one idea?

Stage 5: Edit and Proofread

// Final Check
// Grammar and Language:
• Check verb tenses — do they match throughout?
• Ensure subject-verb agreement
• Watch for run-on sentences
• Check word order in questions

// Vocabulary and Style:
• Have I used varied vocabulary?
• Is the tone appropriate?
• Have I avoided repetition?

// Mechanics:
• Check spelling
• Verify punctuation
• Ensure proper capitalization

📌 Sample Planning Template

💡 Essay Planning Template

Topic: Should smartphones be allowed in schools?


Introduction:

• Hook/Background: Technology is everywhere...

• Thesis statement: I believe smartphones should be allowed with guidelines


Body Paragraph 1:

• Point: Educational benefits

• Example: Apps for learning, research capabilities

• Evidence: Statistics on improved learning


Body Paragraph 2:

• Point: Communication benefits

• Example: Contacting parents, emergencies

• Evidence: Parents' peace of mind


Body Paragraph 3 (Counter-argument):

• Counter: Distractions, misuse

• Rebuttal: With proper supervision and rules


Conclusion:

• Restate thesis: Smartphones should be allowed

• Summary: Benefits outweigh concerns with guidelines

📙 3. Formal and Informal Emails

Email writing is a common task in the BAC exam. Understanding the differences between formal and informal styles is essential.

📌 Formal vs Informal Language

Formal Informal
Dear Sir/Madam Hi, Hello, Hey
Yours sincerely Best, Regards, Love
I am writing to inquire about... I wanted to ask you about...
Furthermore, Moreover Also, Plus
I would appreciate your help Could you help me?
Unfortunately Unfortunately / Sadly
Please find attached Here's the file
Full sentences Contractions OK

📌 Formal Email Structure

📝 Formal Email Format

Subject: Clear, specific subject line

Greeting: Dear Mr./Ms. [Name] or Dear Sir/Madam

Introduction: State your purpose immediately

Body: Provide necessary details in logical order

Conclusion: Thank the reader, restate request if needed

Sign-off: Yours sincerely / Kind regards

Signature: Your name

📌 Formal Email Example

// Formal Email: Job Application
Subject: Application for English Teacher Position

Dear Mr. Johnson,

I am writing to express my interest in the English Teacher position advertised on your school's website.

I graduated from the University of Tunis with a degree in English Literature. I have three years of teaching experience at a secondary school, where I taught students from ages 14 to 18.

I would be grateful if you could provide me with more information about the requirements for this position, including the application deadline and necessary documents.

I have attached my CV and letters of recommendation for your consideration.

I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience.

Yours sincerely,
Ahmed Ben Ali

📌 Another Formal Email Example

// Formal Email: Request for Information
Subject: Request for Information About Summer English Course

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to inquire about your summer English language course for international students.

I am a high school student from Tunisia, and I am interested in improving my English skills during the summer holiday. Could you please provide me with the following information:

1. Course duration and start dates
2. Course fees and payment options
3. Accommodation options
4. Entry requirements

I would be grateful for any additional information you could provide about the program.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to your response.

Yours faithfully,
Sarah Mansour

📌 Informal Email Structure

📝 Informal Email Format

Subject: Casual or no subject needed

Greeting: Hi, Hello, Hey (first name if known)

Body: Conversational, friendly tone

Closing: Best, Take care, Talk soon

Signature: First name only

📌 Informal Email Example

// Informal Email: Catching Up with a Friend
Subject: Hey! Long time no see!

Hi Sara,

How are you doing? I haven't heard from you in ages!

I wanted to tell you that I just got accepted into university! I'm so excited about studying Computer Science. The campus is beautiful, and I've already made some friends.

How's everything going with you? Are you still working at that café? How's your family?

Why don't we meet up next weekend to celebrate? It would be great to catch up! We could go to that coffee shop near the beach.

Let me know when you're free.

Best,
Ahmed

📌 Another Informal Email Example

// Informal Email: Apology to a Friend
Subject: So sorry about yesterday!

Hey Mohammed,

I'm so sorry I couldn't make it to your birthday party yesterday. My car broke down on the way, and I had to wait for a tow truck. It was really frustrating!

How was the party? Did everyone have a good time? I'm really disappointed I missed it. Can we hang out sometime next week? I'll make it up to you!

Sorry again and hope you're not too mad at me.

Take care,
Youssef

⚠️ Key Differences to Remember

• Formal: No contractions (I am, not I'm), complete sentences, polite language

• Informal: Contractions OK, shorter sentences, friendly tone

• Never use: "u" for "you," "gr8" for "great," or text-message abbreviations!

• Always use: proper grammar and spelling in both formal and informal emails

📕 4. Argumentative Essays: Complete Guide

The argumentative essay is the most common and important writing task in the BAC exam. It requires you to present a clear argument with supporting evidence.

📌 Types of Essays

1. Argumentative Essay

Presents a clear position and defends it with evidence and reasoning.

Example: "Technology has done more harm than good to society."

2. Opinion Essay

States your opinion on a topic and supports it with reasons.

Example: "In my opinion, students should learn two foreign languages."

3. Discussion Essay

Discusses both sides of an issue before giving an opinion.

Example: "Some people think..., others believe... In my view..."

4. Problem-Solution Essay

Identifies a problem and proposes solutions.

Example: "Climate change is a serious problem. This essay will discuss solutions..."

📌 Standard 5-Paragraph Essay Structure

📝 The Perfect Essay Structure

Paragraph 1: Introduction

• Hook (attention-grabbing opening)

• Background information

• Thesis statement (main argument)


Paragraph 2: Body 1

• Topic sentence (first main point)

• Explanation and evidence

• Example


Paragraph 3: Body 2

• Topic sentence (second main point)

• Explanation and evidence

• Example


Paragraph 4: Body 3 (Counter-argument)

• Acknowledge opposing view

• Present your response/rebuttal


Paragraph 5: Conclusion

• Restate thesis in different words

• Summary of main points

• Final thought or call to action

📌 Complete Argumentative Essay: Example 1

// Essay: Should smartphones be allowed in schools?

// INTRODUCTION
Introduction:
Smartphones have become an integral part of modern life, especially among young people. With the increasing prevalence of mobile technology, questions arise about whether these devices should be permitted in educational settings. While some argue that smartphones are distracting and harmful, I firmly believe that smartphones should be allowed in schools, provided that proper guidelines are established.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 1
Body Paragraph 1:
First and foremost, smartphones serve as valuable educational tools.
With access to the internet, students can quickly research topics, access educational apps, and learn at their own pace. For example, language learning apps like Duolingo can help students practice foreign languages outside the classroom. Moreover, educational videos on platforms like YouTube can supplement traditional teaching methods and make learning more engaging. Therefore, smartphones can enhance the learning experience and provide students with additional resources.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 2
Body Paragraph 2:
In addition, smartphones enable effective communication between students and parents.
In case of emergencies, students can quickly contact their families to ensure their safety. Similarly, parents can stay informed about their children's schedules and any unexpected changes. This provides peace of mind to both students and parents alike. Furthermore, schools can use messaging apps to send important notifications about schedule changes or events.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 3 (COUNTER-ARGUMENT)
Body Paragraph 3:
Nevertheless, critics argue that smartphones can be distracting and disruptive.
They claim that students may play games, use social media, or text friends instead of focusing on their lessons. However, this issue can be effectively addressed through proper supervision and clear classroom policies. For instance, teachers can implement "phone-free periods" during instruction time or use designated phone storage areas. With proper management, the benefits of smartphones can be maximized while minimizing potential drawbacks.

// CONCLUSION
Conclusion:
In conclusion, smartphones offer significant educational and communicative benefits that should not be overlooked.
While concerns about distraction are valid, they can be mitigated through thoughtful implementation of rules and guidelines. Therefore, I maintain that smartphones should be permitted in schools, as they prepare students for the modern digital world and enhance the educational process when used responsibly.

📌 Complete Argumentative Essay: Example 2

// Essay: The Internet has changed the way we live

// INTRODUCTION
Introduction:
The Internet has undoubtedly transformed nearly every aspect of modern life, from how we communicate to how we work and learn. This essay will discuss both the advantages and disadvantages of this technological revolution. While the Internet has brought remarkable benefits, it also presents significant challenges that must be addressed.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 1 - ADVANTAGES
Body Paragraph 1:
On the one hand, the Internet has made information more accessible than ever before.
Students can now access online libraries, educational resources, and courses from prestigious universities without leaving their homes. For example, platforms like Coursera and Khan Academy offer free courses on virtually any subject. This democratizes education and creates opportunities for lifelong learning. Furthermore, the Internet facilitates instant communication across the globe, allowing people to stay connected with friends and family through video calls and social media.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 2 - ADVANTAGES CONTINUED
Body Paragraph 2:
In addition, the Internet has revolutionized how businesses operate.
E-commerce has enabled companies to reach customers worldwide, while remote work has become increasingly common. This has reduced commuting time and provided flexibility for workers. Similarly, online banking and services have made financial transactions more convenient and efficient.

// BODY PARAGRAPH 3 - DISADVANTAGES
Body Paragraph 3:
On the other hand, the Internet poses serious risks to privacy and security.
Personal information can be stolen through hacking and phishing scams. Moreover, many people fall victim to online fraud and identity theft. Additionally, excessive screen time can lead to health problems such as eye strain, poor posture, and lack of physical activity. As a result, mental health issues like anxiety and depression have increased among heavy Internet users.

// CONCLUSION
Conclusion:
To sum up, the Internet has brought both remarkable benefits and significant challenges to modern society.
While it has transformed education, communication, and commerce, it has also created new threats to privacy and well-being. Therefore, it is essential that we use the Internet responsibly and develop strategies to mitigate its negative effects while continuing to enjoy its numerous advantages.

📒 5. Other Writing Types

Beyond emails and essays, the BAC exam may also require other types of writing. Here is a comprehensive guide to each type.

📌 Article Writing

Articles inform readers about a topic in an engaging, accessible way.

📝 Article Structure

Headline: Catchy, attention-grabbing title

Introduction: Hook the reader, introduce the topic

Body: Key information organized logically

Conclusion: Summary or final thought

// Article: The Importance of Reading

The Power of Reading: Why Books Still Matter in the Digital Age

In an era dominated by smartphones and social media, one might wonder if reading books has become obsolete. However, nothing could be further from the truth. Reading remains one of the most beneficial activities for personal development and intellectual growth.

First and foremost, reading expands our vocabulary and improves our language skills. Unlike social media posts, books provide exposure to rich, varied language. Moreover, reading enhances concentration and critical thinking abilities. For example, novels require us to follow complex narratives and analyze characters' motivations.

Furthermore, reading is an excellent way to reduce stress and escape from daily pressures. According to research, just six minutes of reading can reduce stress levels by up to 68 percent. Whether you enjoy fiction, non-fiction, or poetry, there is a book for everyone.

Therefore, I urge everyone to make reading a regular habit. Visit your local library, join a book club, or simply set aside 30 minutes each day for reading. Your mind will thank you for it!

📌 Summary Writing

A summary condenses the main points of a text into a shorter version.

📝 Summary Writing Rules

• Keep it brief (usually 1/3 of original length)

• Focus on main ideas only

• Use your own words

• Include ONLY information from the text

• No opinions or comments

• Include a thesis statement

// Summary Template
Title/Topic: [Main topic]

Summary:
The text discusses/examines/explores... [Brief overview of main idea].

According to the text, [main point 1]. Additionally, [main point 2]. Finally, [main point 3].

In conclusion, the text emphasizes/highlights/concludes that [final takeaway].

📌 Review Writing

Reviews give opinions about books, movies, restaurants, etc.

📝 Review Structure

Introduction: What you are reviewing, basic information

Summary: Brief overview without spoilers (for books/movies)

Analysis: What works and what doesn't

Opinion: Your overall judgment

Recommendation: Would you recommend it?

// Book/Movie Review Template
Title: [Name of Book/Movie]
Genre: [Type of work]
Rating: ★★★★☆ [4 out of 5 stars]

[Book/Movie Name] is a [type of work] that [brief description]. The story follows [main character] who [brief plot summary].

What I enjoyed most about this [book/movie] was [positive aspect]. However, [negative aspect] could have been improved.

I would recommend this [book/movie] to [target audience]. Overall, it is [final opinion].

📓 6. Linking Words and Transition Phrases

Linking words (connectors or transition words) are essential for creating smooth, coherent writing. They show the relationship between ideas and help your writing flow logically.

📌 Addition (Adding Information)

Connector Usage Example
and Simple addition She is smart and talented.
also Adding similar information She speaks English. She also speaks French.
too Adding at the end She speaks English, French too.
as well Adding similarly She studies hard, as well as playing sports.
in addition (to) Formal addition In addition to studying, she works part-time.
furthermore Strong addition The food was good. Furthermore, the service was excellent.
moreover Adding another point The house is beautiful. Moreover, it has a large garden.
besides Adding an extra point Besides being cheap, it's also delicious.
not only... but also Emphatic addition Not only is it cheap, but also it's healthy.

📌 Contrast (Showing Differences)

Connector Usage Example
but Simple contrast It's cheap, but it's not good.
however Formal contrast It was raining. However, we went out.
nevertheless Formal contrast It was expensive. Nevertheless, I bought it.
although / though / even though Concession Although it was raining, we went out.
despite / in spite of Despite + noun/gerund Despite the rain, we went out.
on the other hand Presenting opposite view City life is exciting. On the other hand, it's expensive.
while / whereas Comparing two things While city life is exciting, rural life is peaceful.
unlike Showing difference Unlike her sister, she is very outgoing.
still Unexpected contrast It was expensive. Still, I bought it.
yet Contrast with positive It was difficult, yet I succeeded.

📌 Cause and Effect

Connector Usage Example
because Reason (before result) I stayed home because it was raining.
since / as Reason (formal) Since you are here, let's begin.
so Result (before reason) It was raining, so I stayed home.
therefore Formal result He studied hard. Therefore, he passed.
consequently Formal result He didn't study. Consequently, he failed.
as a result Result He didn't study. As a result, he failed.
because of / due to Cause (followed by noun) Because of the rain, we stayed home.
owing to Formal cause Owing to the weather, the match was cancelled.

📌 Sequence and Time

Connector Usage Example
first / firstly Beginning First, gather your ingredients.
second / secondly Second point Secondly, mix the dry ingredients.
then / next Next step Then, add water.
after that Following step After that, let it rest for 10 minutes.
afterwards Following Afterwards, we went home.
finally / lastly End point Finally, serve and enjoy.
meanwhile Simultaneous action Meanwhile, prepare the sauce.

📌 Examples and Illustration

Connector Usage Example
for example / for instance Illustrating a point Many jobs require English. For example, in tourism.
such as Giving examples I like fruits such as apples and oranges.
like Informal examples I enjoy sports like football and basketball.
in fact / actually Emphasizing truth In fact, English is the global language.
especially / particularly Focusing on specifics All subjects are important, especially mathematics.

📌 Conclusion and Summary

Connector Usage Example
in conclusion Ending an essay In conclusion, technology has both pros and cons.
to sum up / in summary Summarizing To sum up, we need to take action now.
in short / in brief Concise summary In short, the project was a success.
overall General assessment Overall, it was a great experience.
in my opinion / personally Giving personal view In my opinion, this is the best solution.

📗 8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

Being aware of common errors will help you produce cleaner, more professional writing.

📌 Top 20 Writing Mistakes

⚠️ Mistake 1: Not Answering the Question

Error: Writing about a related topic but not addressing the specific question.

Example: Question asks about effects of technology on education, but you write about technology in general.

Solution: Always read the question carefully and plan your response around it.

⚠️ Mistake 2: No Clear Thesis Statement

Error: Writing without a clear central argument or position.

Solution: State your main argument clearly in the introduction.

⚠️ Mistake 3: Using Informal Language

Error: Using contractions, slang, or casual expressions in formal writing.

Example: "I think smartphones are cool, but they can be distracting."

Better: "While smartphones offer numerous benefits, they can also be distracting."

⚠️ Mistake 4: Repeating Ideas

Error: Saying the same thing in different ways without adding new content.

Solution: Each paragraph should add new information or evidence.

⚠️ Mistake 5: Lack of Examples

Error: Making claims without supporting examples or evidence.

Solution: Always provide specific examples to support your arguments.

⚠️ Mistake 6: Poor Paragraph Structure

Error: Writing without clear paragraph breaks or mixing ideas.

Solution: Each paragraph should focus on one main idea with supporting details.

⚠️ Mistake 7: Weak Conclusions

Error: Introducing new ideas or simply repeating the introduction.

Solution: Restate your thesis, summarize key points, and end with a final thought.

⚠️ Mistake 8: Repetitive Vocabulary

Error: Using the same words repeatedly throughout the essay.

Solution: Use synonyms and varied expressions.

⚠️ Mistake 9: Ignoring the Counter-argument

Error: Only presenting one side without acknowledging opposing views.

Solution: Include a paragraph addressing the opposing view and your response.

⚠️ Mistake 10: Grammar and Spelling Errors

Error: Careless mistakes that reduce the quality of your writing.

Solution: Always proofread your work before submitting.

⚠️ Mistake 11: Word Count Issues

Error: Writing too much or too little.

Solution: Practice writing within the required word limit.

⚠️ Mistake 12: Irrelevant Information

Error: Including off-topic content to fill space.

Solution: Stay focused on the question throughout your response.

⚠️ Mistake 13: Overgeneralization

Error: Using words like "always," "never," "all," "none" when not accurate.

Solution: Use more cautious language: "often," "some," "many," "in some cases."

⚠️ Mistake 14: Missing Transitions

Error: Abrupt jumps between ideas without smooth transitions.

Solution: Use linking words and transition phrases to connect ideas.

⚠️ Mistake 15: Not Following the Format

Error: Writing an email as an essay or vice versa.

Solution: Understand and follow the required format for each task.

📙 9. Evaluation Criteria and Grading

Understanding how your writing is evaluated will help you focus your efforts on what matters most.

📌 Marking Criteria for the BAC Writing

Criterion Weight What Examiners Look For
Content 30% Relevance to the topic, depth of ideas, quality of arguments, supporting evidence
Organization 25% Structure, coherence, paragraph development, logical flow, transitions
Vocabulary 20% Range, accuracy, appropriateness, variety, sophistication
Grammar 15% Accuracy, complexity, range of structures, error frequency
Spelling & Punctuation 10% Accuracy, consistency, appropriate use of punctuation

📌 What Each Band Means

Band 9: Excellent (90-100%)

• Fully addresses all aspects of the task

• Very well organized with perfect paragraphing

• Wide range of vocabulary used accurately

• Complex grammar structures used successfully

• Virtually no errors

Band 7-8: Good (70-89%)

• Addresses all aspects of the task

• Well organized with clear structure

• Good range of vocabulary, few errors

• Good control of grammar, occasional errors

• Minor spelling or punctuation errors

Band 5-6: Average (50-69%)

• Addresses most aspects of the task

• Organized, though may lack coherence

• Adequate vocabulary, some inaccuracies

• Reasonable grammar control, several errors

• Some spelling and punctuation errors

Band 3-4: Below Average (30-49%)

• Partially addresses the task

• Limited organization

• Limited vocabulary range

• Grammar errors impede communication

• Frequent spelling errors

Band 1-2: Poor (0-29%)

• Does not address the task

• Disorganized

• Very limited vocabulary

• Grammar errors make understanding very difficult

• Extensive spelling errors

📌 Tips for Each Criterion

📝

Content: Answer the question fully. Include specific examples. Show depth of thought.

📝

Organization: Use the essay structure. Include clear paragraphs. Use transitions.

📝

Vocabulary: Use synonyms. Avoid repetition. Use topic-specific words.

📝

Grammar: Use varied sentence structures. Check verb tenses. Avoid run-on sentences.

📝

Spelling: Proofread carefully. Be especially careful with commonly misspelled words.

📝

Punctuation: Use commas correctly. Know when to use periods, commas, and semicolons.

📌 Exercise 1: Write a Formal Email

Task

You want to apply for a summer English course at a language school in London. Write a formal email to the school requesting information about the course.

Include:

• Course dates and duration

• Tuition fees

• Accommodation options

• Entry requirements


Model Answer

Subject: Inquiry About Summer English Course

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to inquire about your summer English language course for international students.

I am a high school student from Tunisia, and I am interested in improving my English skills during the summer holidays. Could you please provide me with the following information:

1. Course dates and duration

2. Tuition fees and payment options

3. Accommodation options and costs

4. Entry requirements for international students

I would be grateful if you could also send me any brochures or application forms that might be relevant.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you.

Yours faithfully,

Mohamed Trabelsi

📌 Exercise 2: Write an Argumentative Essay

Task

Topic: "The Internet has done more harm than good to society."

Write an argumentative essay presenting your point of view. Write at least 150 words.


Model Answer

Introduction:

The Internet has undoubtedly transformed the way we live, work, and communicate. However, there is ongoing debate about whether its impact has been largely positive or negative. In my opinion, while the Internet has brought many benefits, I believe it has also caused significant harm to society.


Body Paragraph 1:

First and foremost, the Internet has made communication faster and more convenient. People can now connect with others across the globe instantly through social media and video calls. For example, families separated by distance can now see each other regularly through platforms like Skype. Moreover, businesses can operate globally and efficiently.


Body Paragraph 2:

Nevertheless, the Internet has also had detrimental effects on mental health. Studies have shown that excessive social media use can lead to anxiety, depression, and loneliness, particularly among young people. Furthermore, the spread of misinformation and cyberbullying has become increasingly prevalent, causing harm to individuals and society as a whole.


Conclusion:

To conclude, the Internet presents both advantages and disadvantages. While it has revolutionized communication and access to information, its negative effects on mental health and social well-being cannot be ignored. Therefore, it is essential that we use the Internet responsibly and develop strategies to mitigate its harmful effects while continuing to enjoy its many benefits.

📌 Exercise 3: Complete with Linking Words

Task

Complete the following paragraph with appropriate linking words.


"Social media has become ubiquitous in modern society. _______ (1) it offers many benefits, _______ (2) there are also concerns about its impact. _______ (3) the ability to stay connected with friends and family, many people spend too much time scrolling through feeds. _______ (4), this can lead to addiction and mental health issues. _______ (5), experts recommend setting boundaries and using social media mindfully. _______ (6), individuals should be aware of the potential risks _______ (7) they can enjoy the benefits safely."


Answers

1. Although / While

2. there are also / however

3. Despite / In spite of

4. Consequently / As a result

5. Therefore / Thus

6. In conclusion / To sum up

7. so that / in order to

📌 Exercise 4: Improve the Vocabulary

Task

Rewrite the following sentences using more sophisticated vocabulary.


1. The Internet is very important today.

→ The Internet has become essential/vital/crucial in today's world.


2. Many people use the Internet for learning.

→ The Internet is widely utilized/harnessed for educational purposes/learning.


3. Social media is a good thing.

→ Social media offers significant/substantial benefits/advantages.


4. This problem is very big.

→ This is a major/significant/substantial issue.


5. The Internet can cause health problems.

→ The Internet can lead to/result in/cause various health issues/problems.

📌 Exercise 5: Write a Summary

Task

Read the following paragraph and write a summary in no more than 50 words.


Original Text:

"Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges facing humanity today. Rising global temperatures have led to melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and more frequent extreme weather events. Scientists warn that without immediate action, the situation will only worsen. Governments around the world are implementing policies to reduce carbon emissions and promote renewable energy sources."


Model Summary (47 words)

Climate change is a major global challenge. Rising temperatures have caused melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and extreme weather. Scientists warn that without immediate action, the situation will worsen. Governments are implementing policies to reduce carbon emissions and promote renewable energy to address this crisis.
